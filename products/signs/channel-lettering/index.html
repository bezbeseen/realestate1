Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between front-lit and back-lit?

Front-lit (or standard) channel letters emit light through the colored acrylic face of the letter. Back-lit (or halo-lit) letters are opaque on the face and emit light from behind, creating a glowing halo effect on the mounting surface.

How are channel letters mounted?

They can be mounted in two ways: directly to the building facade (flush mount) or on a raceway, which is a metal box that houses the power supplies and wiring.

Are LED lights used?

Yes, we exclusively use energy-efficient, low-voltage LED modules in our illuminated channel letters. They are long-lasting, require minimal maintenance, and save on energy costs compared to traditional neon.
